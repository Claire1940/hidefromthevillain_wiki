<Section icon={Target} title="Essential Hide From The Villain Tips for Survivors">

To consistently win rounds, you must adopt a proactive survival mindset. Applying specific **hide from the villain tips** will help you navigate the pressure of being hunted by powerful skins like Homelander or Omni-Man. The villain has superior speed and visibility, so your survival depends entirely on outsmarting them through positioning.

One of the most overlooked strategies is "Exit Awareness." Many beginners find a dark corner and stay there until they are discovered. A pro survivor never enters a room without knowing exactly how they will leave it if the villain enters. This means avoiding dead-end hallways and small rooms with only one door unless you have a TEMP V power ready for a quick getaway.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Positioning">
    At the start of the round, move away from the spawn point immediately. Villains often check the closest rooms first. Find a location with multiple line-of-sight breaks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The 3-Second Rule">
    When you hear the villain's heartbeat or audio cues, you have roughly three seconds to break line-of-sight and find cover. Do not wait to see the villain before moving.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Active Repositioning">
    Once a villain searches your area and leaves, do not stay in the same spot. Villains frequently backtrack to areas they previously cleared to catch complacent survivors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Power Preparation">
    If you find TEMP V, do not use it immediately. Keep it as a "get out of jail free" card for when you are cornered or during the final rescue sprint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Stacking">
Do not hide in the same room as three other survivors. A single sweep by the villain will result in multiple eliminations, and the noise generated by a group is much easier for the villain to track.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering TEMP V Powers and Combat">

TEMP V is the defining mechanic that separates Hide From The Villain from standard horror survival games. When you consume TEMP V, you are granted a random superhero ability. These powers are not meant to let you fight the villain head-on for the entire round; rather, they are tools to disrupt the villain's hunt and create windows of opportunity for your team.

Using these powers correctly requires timing. If you activate a speed boost power in an open field, you are simply a faster target. If you use it to navigate a complex maze of rooms, you can lose the villain entirely. Learning the specific utility of each random power is key to high-level play.

| Power Category | Primary Use | Best Timing | Survival Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mobility** | Escaping a chase | When the villain is within 10 studs | **Excellent** |
| **Disruption** | Stun or knockback | To save a trapped teammate | **Good** |
| **Defense** | Absorbing damage | During the final rescue sprint | **High** |
| **Utility** | Scouting or stealth | When the villain's location is unknown | **Moderate** |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Map Awareness and Hiding Behavior">

While maps in Hide From The Villain can change with updates, the fundamental principles of map awareness remain constant. Every environment is designed with "Hot Zones" and "Safe Zones." Hot Zones are areas with high visibility or high traffic, such as central lobbies or spawn points. Safe Zones are tucked-away rooms or areas with complex layouts that make it difficult for a villain to search quickly.

Your movement through the map should be deliberate. Instead of running aimlessly, move from one piece of cover to the next. This "Cover-to-Cover" movement ensures that if the villain suddenly appears, you are never more than a second away from a hiding spot.

| Hiding Behavior | Risk | Reward | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Static Hiding** | High | Low | When the villain is in the next room |
| **Dynamic Hiding** | Low | High | Standard gameplay loop |
| **Baiting** | Extreme | High | Distracting the villain from the rescue zone |
| **Edge-Hugging** | Medium | Medium | Navigating between safe zones |
